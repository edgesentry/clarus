Singapore Infrastructure Protection Act (Cap. 136A), Section 18 — Entry into Protected Areas

Designated protected areas in Singapore waters include petrochemical terminal approaches (Jurong Island), naval facility waters (Tuas, Sembawang), liquefied natural gas terminal exclusion zones, and fairway clearance zones activated during special operations.

Entry into a protected area without written authorisation from the Commissioner of Police or the relevant protected area owner is a criminal offence. Vessels that enter or attempt to enter a protected area are subject to:
- Detention by the Singapore Police Coast Guard
- Fine not exceeding SGD 500,000 or imprisonment not exceeding 10 years, or both
- Immediate port state control inspection on arrival

MPA Port Marine Notice No. 63 of 2024 specifies the chart coordinates of all active restricted zones within Singapore port limits. Mariners are required to maintain awareness of restricted zone boundaries and must not approach within the indicated safety margin without prior authorisation.

Under the Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore Act (Cap. 170A), the Port Master may direct any vessel within port limits to alter course if it is assessed to be on an approach track toward a restricted zone.

This rule fires when a vessel entity enters the restricted zone polygon as defined in rules.json. The polygon represents the zone boundary in metres relative to the chart origin configured for this deployment. In production, zone coordinates should be updated from the current MPA Port Marine Notice to reflect active restricted zone boundaries.
